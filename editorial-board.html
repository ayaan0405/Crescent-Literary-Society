Reporting & Editing

The Quiet Craft Behind Every Word

Writing takes shape through two quiet yet powerful forces: reporting and editing. Reporting is all about observing, exploring, and sharing stories that matter. Editing is the quiet craft that hones writing and brings impact to those stories — shaping writing into what the writer truly meant, through thoughtful choices and careful precision.

Our board handles two major projects: Crescent Line and Meraki. Whether it's covering events, crafting interviews, or curating art and thought — our work is a blend of insight and intention.

Here, words are not just written. They are nurtured, refined, set to taint and leave a mark.

Our Publications

📰
Dean's Newsletter

Crescent Line

The Crescent Line is CLS's official campus chronicle — the Dean's newsletter published and managed entirely by the Editorial Board. From event coverage and interviews to opinion and campus culture, Crescent Line documents the pulse of the institution with precision and care.

Our reporters observe, explore, and bring stories that matter to the Crescent community. Our editors shape those stories into something lasting.

📖
Bi-Annual Magazine

Meraki

Meraki (Greek: to do something with soul, creativity, or love) is CLS's bi-annual creative and reflective magazine. Published twice a year, Meraki is a curated blend of student writing, artwork, and thought — capturing the emotional and intellectual life of the Crescent community.

Creative essays, poetry, interviews, art features, and cultural commentary — Meraki is where expression and intention meet.
